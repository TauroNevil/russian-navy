Supreme Commander-in-Chief · Russian Armed Forces
Vladimir Vladimirovich Putin

Vladimir Putin serves as the Supreme Commander-in-Chief of the Russian Armed Forces, a role enshrined in the Russian Constitution. As President of the Russian Federation, he holds ultimate authority over all five fleet commands of the Russian Navy. Putin has consistently prioritized naval modernization, overseeing the deployment of hypersonic Zircon missiles, new Borei-A ballistic missile submarines, and the expansion of Kalibr cruise missile capability across the fleet.

— HISTORY

TIMELINE

1696
Foundation by Peter the Great
Tsar Peter I creates the Russian Navy. The Azov Fleet contests Ottoman control of the Black Sea.
1714
Battle of Gangut
Russia's first major naval victory defeats Sweden, securing Baltic dominance.
1770
Battle of Chesma
Russian squadron burns the Ottoman fleet in the Aegean — 15 ships of the line destroyed.
1853
Battle of Sinop
Nakhimov destroys the Ottoman fleet. Last major all-sail naval engagement in history.
1905
Battle of Tsushima
Japan destroys Russia's Baltic Fleet — 21 ships lost. Russia's worst naval defeat.
1941–45
World War II
Soviet Navy defends Leningrad, sustains Arctic convoys, and operates in all four sea theaters.
1950s–80s
Cold War Expansion
Under Admiral Gorshkov, the Soviet Navy becomes a true global blue-water force.
2015–Present
Kalibr & Zircon Era
Long-range hypersonic missiles transform small ships and submarines into strategic strike platforms.
